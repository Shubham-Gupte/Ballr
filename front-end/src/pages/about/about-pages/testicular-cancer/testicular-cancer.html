Testicular Cancer

The symptoms of testicular cancer include:
-Lumps or bumps in the testes that weren't there before
-Pain in the testes or the lower abdomen
-Feeling of "heaviness" in the scrotum
-Breast/Chest enlargement
-Lower back pain

If the cancer spreads along the lymph nodes, it can cause symptoms like:
-Hemoptysis (coughing up blood)
-A lump in the neck

These symptoms can be scary and painful, which is why it is so important to keep track of your reproductive health.
Treatments for testicular cancer include orchiectomy (removal of the testicle(s)), chemotherapy, and radiation.
